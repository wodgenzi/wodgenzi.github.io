Chapter 3: Motion and Force

  • Introduction

    Show/Hide Details
    • Motion/rest: relative to observer.
    • Rest: position unchanged w.r.t. surroundings.
    • Motion: position changes w.r.t. surroundings.
    A moving body can be both at rest and in motion, depending on the observer.

  • Displacement

    Show/Hide Details
    • Vector: change in position (initial to final).

    • Distance

      Show/Hide Details
      • Length of path between two points.
      For straight-line motion, displacement equals distance.

  • Speed & Velocity

    Show/Hide Details
    • Velocity: Time rate of change of displacement (Vector).
    • Speed: Magnitude of velocity (Scalar).

    • Speed

      Show/Hide Details
      • Ratio: distance / time.
      • Scalar.
      • Dimensions: $[LT^{-1}]$.
      • Unit: $m~s^{-1}$.

    • Velocity

      Show/Hide Details
      • Time rate of change of displacement.
      • Vector.
    For uniform speed, instantaneous and average speeds are equal. Relative velocity: e.g., boat in current.

  • Acceleration

    Show/Hide Details
    • Rate of change of velocity: $\vec{a} = \frac{\Delta \vec{v}}{\Delta t}$.
    • Produced by change in:
      • Magnitude of velocity (speed).
      • Direction of velocity.
      • Both magnitude and direction.
    • Vector.
    • Unit: $m/s^2$.
    • Dimensions: $[LT^{-2}]$.
    • Deceleration: velocity reduces (not zero).
    • Retardation: velocity reduces to zero.

    • Types of Acceleration

      Show/Hide Details
      • Uniform
      • Variable
      • Average
      • Instantaneous
      • Positive
      • Due to gravity
      • Negative (retardation)

  • Graphs (Motion)

    Show/Hide Details

    • Displacement-Time Graph

      Show/Hide Details
      • Slope = Velocity.
      • Parallel to x-axis: rest, zero velocity.
      • Inclined straight line: constant velocity.
      • Parallel to y-axis: infinite velocity (not possible).
      • Increasing slope: increasing velocity, accelerated motion.
      • Decreasing slope: decreasing velocity, decelerated motion.

    • Velocity-Time Graph

      Show/Hide Details
      • Slope = Acceleration: $a = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} = \tan\theta$.
      • Horizontal line: constant velocity, zero acceleration.
      • Inclined line: constant acceleration.
      • Curved line: variable acceleration.

    • Important Points (Velocity-Time Graph)

      Show/Hide Details
      • Constant velocity $\implies$ zero acceleration/force.
      • Body falls freely/starts from rest: $v_i = 0$.
      • Velocity and acceleration not necessarily in same direction.
      • Velocity and acceleration not necessarily zero simultaneously.
      • Average speed (equal distance, $v_1, v_2$): $v_{av} = \frac{2v_1v_2}{v_1+v_2}$.
      • Average speed (equal time, $v_1, v_2$): $speed = \frac{v_1+v_2}{2}$.
      • Body thrown upward, $v_f=0$ at height $h$: $h = \frac{v^2}{2g}$.
      • Average acceleration ($a_1$ for $t_1$, $a_2$ for $t_2$): $a_{av} = \frac{a_1t_1+a_2t_2}{t_1+t_2}$.

  • Equations of Motion (by Galileo)

    Show/Hide Details
    • $v_f = v_i + at$ (General) or $v_f = v_i + gt$ (Vertical)
    • $s = v_i t + \frac{1}{2}at^2$ (General) or $s = v_i t + \frac{1}{2}gt^2$ (Vertical)
    • $v_f^2 = v_i^2 + 2as$ (General) or $v_f^2 = v_i^2 + 2gs$ (Vertical)
    • Distance in $n^{th}$ second: $s_n = v_i + \frac{1}{2}a(2n-1)$.

  • Newton's Laws of Motion

    Show/Hide Details
    • From Newton's PRINCIPIA.
    • Applicable for speeds not comparable to light speed.

    • 1st Law (Law of Inertia)

      Show/Hide Details
      • Body maintains rest/uniform motion unless external force acts.
      • Qualitatively defines force.
      • Inertia: inability to change state of motion.

    • 2nd Law (Force and Acceleration)

      Show/Hide Details
      • Force causes acceleration in its direction.
      • $F \propto ma \implies F = ma$ (if $k=1$).
      • $F = \frac{\Delta p}{\Delta t}$ (change in momentum per second).
      • Weight: Earth's gravitational force on a body.

    • 3rd Law (Action and Reaction)

      Show/Hide Details
      • Every action has equal and opposite reaction.
      • $\vec{F}_{AB} = -\vec{F}_{BA}$ or $\vec{F}_{action} = -\vec{F}_{reaction}$.
      • Action/reaction forces act on *different* bodies.

  • Inertial and Non-Inertial Frame

    Show/Hide Details

    • Inertial Frame

      Show/Hide Details
      • Body at rest or uniform velocity ($\vec{a}=\vec{0}$).
      • Law of inertia holds.

    • Non-Inertial Frame

      Show/Hide Details
      • Not uniform velocity ($\vec{a}\ne\vec{0}$).
      • Law of inertia does not hold.

  • Momentum

    Show/Hide Details

    • Linear Momentum

      Show/Hide Details
      • Product of mass and velocity: $\vec{p} = m\vec{v}$.
      • Vector, points along velocity.
      • $\vec{p} = \vec{0}$ if $\vec{v}=\vec{0}$.
      • SI unit: $kg~m~s^{-1}$ or $Ns$.
      • Dimension: $[MLT^{-1}]$.

  • Impulse

    Show/Hide Details
    • Product of force and duration: $\vec{I} = \Delta \vec{p} = \vec{F}\Delta t$.
    • Same dimensions as linear momentum.
    • Change in momentum = Impulse.
    Thermore pieces protect dinnerware by increasing impact time (reducing force).

  • Law of Conservation of Linear Momentum

    Show/Hide Details
    • In isolated system, total linear momentum is constant: $m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = m_1v_1' + m_2v_2'$.

    • Collisions

      Show/Hide Details
      • Interaction due to closeness.

      Elastic Collision in One Dimension

      • Bodies remain on line of motion.
      • Speed of approach = Speed of recession: $v_1 - v_2 = -(v_1' - v_2')$.
      • After collision velocities:
        • $v_1' = \frac{(m_1 - m_2)v_1 + 2m_2v_2}{(m_1 + m_2)}$
        • $v_2' = \frac{(m_2 - m_1)v_2 + 2m_1v_1}{(m_1 + m_2)}$

      Cases of Elastic Collision:

      Case Condition Result
      I $m_1=m_2$ $v_1'=v_2$ & $v_2'=v_1$
      II $m_1=m_2$ & $v_2=0$ $v_1'=0$ & $v_2'=v_1$
      Velocities interchange in cases I & II.
      III $m_1 \gg m_2$ & $v_2=0$ $v_1'=v_1$ & $v_2'=2v_1$
      IV $m_1 \ll m_2$ & $v_2=0$ $v_1'=-v$ & $v_2'=0$

  • Force Due to Water Flow

    Show/Hide Details
    • Force exerted by water on wall: $F = \frac{m}{t}v$.
    • Basis for turbines (hydral energy).

  • Momentum & Explosive Forces

    Show/Hide Details
    • Conservation of momentum governs explosion fragments.
    • Recoil velocity of rifle: $v' = -\frac{m}{M}v$.
    • Recoil velocity is small fraction of bullet velocity ($M \gg m$).

  • Propulsion of Rocket

    Show/Hide Details
    • Based on conservation of linear momentum.
    • Fuel: ~80% of launch mass.
    • Fuel consumption: ~10,000 Kg/s.
    • Exhaust gas speed: >4000 m/s.
    • Instantaneous acceleration: $a = mv/M$.
    Multi-compartment rockets drop empty sections to reduce mass and increase acceleration.

  • Friction

    Show/Hide Details
    • Resisting force between rough bodies, opposes sliding.
    • Acts opposite to tendency of motion.
    • Max friction depends on: nature of surfaces, normal force.
    • Independent of contact area (within limits).
    • Decreases once motion starts.
    • Never zero in practice.
    • Walking: friction acts forward (opposes backward slip).
    • Caused by molecular interlocking/irregularities.

    • Coefficient of friction ($\mu$)

      Show/Hide Details
      • $\mu = \frac{\text{Limiting friction (f)}}{\text{Normal reaction (R)}}$ or $f = \mu R$.
      • Depends on material, surface finish, film, temperature.
      • Horizontal surface: $R = mg \implies f = \mu mg$.

    • Static Friction

      Show/Hide Details
      • Force before actual motion starts.
      • $F_s = \mu_s R_s$.

    • Kinetic Friction

      Show/Hide Details
      • Force during motion.
      • $F_k = \mu_k R_k$.
      • Limiting friction: max force before sliding.

    • Angle of Friction ($\theta$)

      Show/Hide Details
      • Angle of resultant of normal reaction (R) and limiting friction (f) with R.
      • $\tan\theta = \frac{f}{R}$ or $\mu = \tan\theta$.
      • Static: $\tan\theta_s = \mu_s$.
      • Kinetic: $\tan\theta_k = \mu_k$.
      • Note: $\mu_s > \mu_k$.

  • Masses in Contact/Connected by Strings

    Show/Hide Details

    • 1) Masses in Contact

      Show/Hide Details
      • $F=$ Horizontal force on $m_1$. $F_c=$ Contact force. $a=$ Acceleration.
      • $F - F_c = m_1a$
      • $F_c = m_2a$
      • Result: $F_c = \frac{m_2F}{m_1+m_2}$, $a = \frac{F}{m_1+m_2}$.

    • 2) Masses Connected by Strings

      Show/Hide Details
      • $F=$ Force on $m_1$. $T=$ Tension. $a=$ Acceleration.
      • $F - T = m_1a$
      • $T = m_2a$
      • Result: $T = \frac{m_2F}{m_1+m_2}$, $a = \frac{F}{m_1+m_2}$.
      • For three masses ($m_1, m_2, m_3$):
      • $F - T_1 = m_1a$
      • $T_1 - T_2 = m_2a$
      • $T_2 = m_3a$
      • Result: $a = \frac{F}{m_1+m_2+m_3}$, $T_2 = \frac{m_3F}{m_1+m_2+m_3}$, $T_1 = \frac{(m_2+m_3)F}{m_1+m_2+m_3}$.

  • Tension and Acceleration in a String (Pulley Systems)

    Show/Hide Details

    • Case 1: Vertical motion (two bodies, frictionless pulley)

      Show/Hide Details
      • If $m_1 > m_2$, $m_1$ moves down, $m_2$ moves up with same acceleration.
      • Equations: $m_1g - T = m_1a$, $T - m_2g = m_2a$.
      • Result: $a = \frac{m_1 - m_2}{m_1 + m_2}g$, $T = \frac{2m_1m_2}{m_1 + m_2}g$.

    • Case 2: One vertical, other horizontal

      Show/Hide Details
      • Acceleration: $a = \frac{m_1g}{m_1+m_2}$.
      • Tension: $T = \frac{m_1m_2g}{m_1+m_2}$.

  • Mechanical Advantage

    Show/Hide Details
    • Ratio of load to effort.
    • Mechanical Advantage = $\frac{\text{LOAD}}{\text{EFFORT}}$
    • LOAD = MECHANICAL ADVANTAGE $\times$ EFFORT
    • EFFORT = $\frac{\text{LOAD}}{\text{MECHANICAL ADVANTAGE}}$

  • Projectile Motion

    Show/Hide Details
    • Instantaneous velocity: max at projection/landing, min at max height ($v_y=0$).
    • Horizontal velocity component: constant.
    • Vertical velocity component: decreases to zero (max height), then increases.
    • Trajectory: Parabola (no air friction).

    • Assumptions

      Show/Hide Details
      • Earth is flat.
      • Gravity (g) constant.
      • Negligible aerodynamic effects.

    • Coordinates at time 't'

      Show/Hide Details
      • $x = v_o \cos\theta_o~t$
      • $y = v_o \sin\theta_o~t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2$

    • Time to reach Maximum Height

      Show/Hide Details
      • $t = \frac{v_o \sin\theta_o}{g}$

    • Total Time of Flight

      Show/Hide Details
      • $T = \frac{2v_o \sin\theta_o}{g}$

    • Vertical Range (Maximum Height)

      Show/Hide Details
      • $H = \frac{v_o^2 \sin^2\theta_o}{2g}$

    • Horizontal Range

      Show/Hide Details
      • $R = \frac{v_o^2 \sin2\theta_o}{g}$ or $R = \frac{v_o^2 (2\sin\theta_o \cos\theta_o)}{g}$
      • Maximum range at $\theta = 45^\circ$: $R_{max} = \frac{v_o^2}{g}$.
      • Relation between range and height: $R \tan\theta = 4H$.
      • Equal ranges for $\theta_1 + \theta_2 = 90^\circ$.
      Range = Max Height when angle of projection is $76^\circ$.

    • Ballistic Missile Application

      Show/Hide Details
      • Un-powered, un-guided.
      • Air friction affects motion.
      • Short range only.
      • Long range/precision: powered, remote-controlled guided missiles.

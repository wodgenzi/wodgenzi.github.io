Chapter 4: Work and Energy

  • Work Done by a Constant Force

    Show/Hide Details
    • Work: Scalar product of force and displacement.
    • $W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{d} = Fd \cos\theta$.
    • Unit: Joule (J) = N m.
    • Dimensions: $[ML^2T^{-2}]$.
    • Work is a scalar quantity.

    • Conditions for Work Done

      Show/Hide Details
      • Force applied.
      • Displacement occurs.
      • Force component along displacement.

    • Cases of Work Done

      Show/Hide Details
      Angle ($\theta$) $\cos\theta$ Work Done (W) Description
      $0^\circ$ 1 $Fd$ (Maximum positive) Force and displacement parallel.
      $90^\circ$ 0 0 Force and displacement perpendicular.
      $180^\circ$ -1 $-Fd$ (Maximum negative) Force and displacement anti-parallel.
      Work done by centripetal force is always zero. Work done by gravitational force on satellite is zero.

  • Work Done by a Variable Force

    Show/Hide Details
    • If force changes, work is calculated by dividing displacement into small elements.
    • Total work = sum of work done over each element.
    • Graphically: Area under Force-Displacement graph.

  • Work Done by Gravitational Field

    Show/Hide Details
    • Work done by gravitational field is independent of path.
    • $W_{gravity} = mgh$.
    • Work done by gravitational field in a closed path is zero.

  • Power

    Show/Hide Details
    • Rate of doing work.
    • $P = \frac{W}{t}$.
    • Unit: Watt (W) = J/s.
    • Dimensions: $[ML^2T^{-3}]$.
    • Scalar quantity.
    • Instantaneous Power (linear): $P = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{v} = Fv \cos\theta$.
    • Instantaneous Power (angular): $P = \vec{\tau} \cdot \vec{\omega} = \tau\omega \cos\theta$.
    • 1 Horsepower (hp) = 746 W.

  • Energy

    Show/Hide Details
    • Capacity to do work.
    • Unit: Joule (J).
    • Dimensions: $[ML^2T^{-2}]$ (same as work).
    • Scalar quantity.

    • Kinetic Energy (KE)

      Show/Hide Details
      • Energy due to motion.
      • $KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$.
      • KE is always positive.
      • Relation with momentum: $KE = \frac{p^2}{2m}$.
      • Percentage change in KE if momentum changes: $\%\Delta KE = (2 + \%\Delta p)\%\Delta p$. (Approximation for small changes)
      • Percentage change in momentum if KE changes: $\%\Delta p = \frac{1}{2}\%\Delta KE$. (Approximation for small changes)

    • Potential Energy (PE)

      Show/Hide Details
      • Energy due to position or configuration.

      Gravitational Potential Energy (GPE)

      • $GPE = mgh$.
      • Depends on reference level.
      • Can be positive, negative, or zero.

      Elastic Potential Energy (EPE)

      • Energy stored in a stretched/compressed spring.
      • $EPE = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$.
      • $k$: spring constant (N/m).
      • $x$: displacement from equilibrium.

  • Work-Energy Theorem

    Show/Hide Details
    • Net work done on a body = Change in its kinetic energy.
    • $W_{net} = \Delta KE = KE_f - KE_i = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 - \frac{1}{2}mv_i^2$.

  • Conservation of Energy

    Show/Hide Details
    • Total energy of an isolated system remains constant.
    • Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.
    • $KE_i + PE_i = KE_f + PE_f$.
    • Total Mechanical Energy ($TME$) = $KE + PE$.
    • $TME$ is conserved in conservative fields (e.g., gravity, elastic).

    • Conservative and Non-Conservative Forces

      Show/Hide Details
      Conservative Force Non-Conservative Force
      Work done independent of path. Work done depends on path.
      Work done in closed path is zero. Work done in closed path is non-zero.
      Associated with potential energy. Not associated with potential energy.
      e.g., Gravitational, Elastic, Electrostatic. e.g., Friction, Air drag.

  • Efficiency

    Show/Hide Details
    • Ratio of useful output to total input.
    • Efficiency ($\eta$) = $\frac{\text{Output}}{\text{Input}} \times 100\%$.
    • Always less than 100% for real machines.

  • Interconversion of Mass and Energy

    Show/Hide Details
    • Einstein's mass-energy equivalence: $E = mc^2$.
    • $c$: speed of light ($3 \times 10^8$ m/s).
    • Small mass conversion yields huge energy.

  • Absolute Gravitational Potential Energy

    Show/Hide Details
    • Definition: Work done by gravitational force in moving a body from infinity to a point in the gravitational field.
    • Formula: $U = -\frac{GMm}{r}$.
    • $G$: Universal gravitational constant ($6.67 \times 10^{-11} N m^2 kg^{-2}$).
    • $M$: Mass of the Earth.
    • $m$: Mass of the object.
    • $r$: Distance from the center of the Earth.
    • Always negative (attractive force, zero at infinity).

  • Escape Velocity

    Show/Hide Details
    • Definition: Minimum velocity required for an object to escape Earth's gravitational field (or any celestial body's) and never return.
    • Formula: $v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{R}}$ or $v_e = \sqrt{2gR}$.
    • $G$: Universal gravitational constant.
    • $M$: Mass of the Earth.
    • $R$: Radius of the Earth.
    • $g$: Acceleration due to gravity on Earth's surface.
    • For Earth: $v_e \approx 11.2~km/s$.
    • Independent of the mass or direction of projection of the escaping object.
